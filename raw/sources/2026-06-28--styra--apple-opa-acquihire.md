Good for: Styra/Apple OPA acqui-hire (Aug 2025); Styra funding history; OPA-CNCF relationship.

- Aug 2025 (post published Aug 20, 2025): Apple hired the core OPA maintainers — Teemu Koponen, Tim Hinrichs, Torin Sandall (the three OPA creators) — plus several Styra engineering staff.
- Characterized as an "acqui-hire," NOT a full acquisition of the Styra corporate entity.
- Styra's commercial offerings around OPA (e.g. Styra DAS — Declarative Authorization Service) face an uncertain future; "will no longer be actively developed in the same way." Some enterprise tools (EOPA / Enterprise OPA, SDKs, Regal linter) reported elsewhere as moving to open source.
- OPA itself: remains a CNCF graduated project; governance/license unchanged; community continues to steward the engine; Apple committed to same monthly release cadence (per other coverage).
- Comparisons drawn to Apple's 2015 FoundationDB acqui-hire (went dark ~3 years).

Styra funding history (TechCrunch / Styra press):
- Founded 2015; created Open Policy Agent (donated to CNCF 2018).
- Series A: $14M led by Accel (with Unusual Ventures, A.Capital).
- Series B: $40M (May 2021) led by Battery Ventures (Accel, Unusual, A.Capital, CapitalOne Ventures, Citi Ventures).
- HQ: Redwood City, CA.
