Zscaler, Inc. (NASDAQ: ZS), publicly traded cloud security company, HQ San Jose, California.

Announcement date: January 27, 2026 — "Zscaler Unveils New Innovations to Secure Enterprise AI Adoption."

### AI Security Suite — three core capabilities (vendor framing / marketing):
1. AI Asset Management — comprehensive inventory of AI apps, models, infrastructure, agents, and usage; detect shadow AI; understand what data AI touches; prioritize risk.
2. Secure Access to AI — safely enable sanctioned AI services with Zero Trust controls, inline inspection, and prompt classification to reduce data loss/misuse.
3. Secure AI Infrastructure and Apps — automated AI red teaming, prompt hardening, runtime guardrails, continuous risk posture from build to runtime.

Comprehensive data protection: prevent leakage of sensitive IP/customer data through prompts, model training inputs, or AI-to-AI interactions, both inline and out-of-band.

Governance alignment cited: NIST AI Risk Management Framework and EU AI Act.

### AI Access Security product page (marketing) features:
- Shadow AI detection: "Detect and classify thousands of AI apps including AI embedded in popular SaaS applications." Dashboard visibility into users, departments, app trends.
- Access control: "Allow, block, or coach access by users or user groups."
- Inline DLP: "Block the loss of sensitive data in prompts" across "100+ DLP dictionaries like Source Code, PII, PCI, PHI, and more."
- Content moderation: detect off-topic/policy-violating use (toxic, restricted, competitive topics) by analyzing prompts and responses.
- Deployment: repeated references to "inline" controls / "inline DLP" → proxy-based inspection architecture (consistent with Zscaler's cloud proxy / Zero Trust Exchange).
- Browser isolation enforcement option.
