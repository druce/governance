## Key extracted facts

- CrowdStrike announced definitive agreement to acquire Pangea on 2025-09-16, at Fal.Con 2025 (Las Vegas).
- Reported deal value ~$260M (BankInfoSecurity). Not disclosed in the press release itself.
- Acquisition completed late September 2025 (CrowdStrike: "Today we have officially completed our acquisition of Pangea" — ~2025-09-26).
- Purpose: extend the Falcon platform to deliver "the industry's first complete AI Detection and Response (AIDR)" — securing data, models, agents, identities, infrastructure, and interactions across the AI lifecycle (development through workforce usage).
- George Kurtz (CrowdStrike CEO): "AI is rewriting the enterprise attack surface at breakneck speed. Each prompt becomes an entry point for the adversary. With Pangea, CrowdStrike will secure the entire AI lifecycle..."
- Pangea capabilities: AI prompt-layer security / AI guardrails; prompt-injection blocking (claimed 99% efficacy at sub-30ms latency — vendor marketing); risky-AI-use prevention; secure AI development. Deployed via gateways or embedded in applications with a few lines of code (API/SDK).

## Pangea background
- Founded 2021. HQ Palo Alto, California.
- Co-founders Oliver Friedrichs (CEO) and Sourabh Satish — previously founders of Phantom Cyber (SOAR), acquired by Splunk 2018.
- Funding: ~$51M total. $26M Series B (Nov 2022) led by GV, with Decibel and Okta Ventures.
- Originally a developer-first security-services API platform; pivoted to "AI Guardrail Platform" for AI security.
