Good for: Confirming Veeam's acquisition of Securiti AI — price, dates, deal close, and what Securiti does.

## Key facts
- Acquirer: Veeam Software (data resilience/backup leader; Insight Partners-owned).
- Target: Securiti AI (DSPM, privacy, governance, data access, AI trust).
- Price: $1.725 billion.
- Announced: 2025-10-21 (Bloomberg: "Insight-Owned Veeam to Acquire Securiti AI for $1.7 Billion").
- Completed/closed: 2025-12-11 ("Veeam Completes Acquisition of Securiti AI to Create the Industry's First Trusted Data Platform for Accelerating Safe AI at Scale").
- Securiti CEO Rehan Jalil joined Veeam as President of Security and AI.
- ~600 Securiti employees joined Veeam. Further platform integration rolling out through 2026.

## What Securiti does
- Pioneered the "Data Command Center" — a knowledge-graph-powered platform unifying data intelligence, governance, and protection across hybrid, multicloud, and SaaS.
- DSPM: discover, classify, and secure sensitive data; privacy (PrivacyOps), data access governance, and AI trust/safe enterprise AI search; agentic AI for automation.
- Founded 2019, HQ San Jose, CA. Founder/CEO Rehan Jalil (prior: Elastica, sold to Symantec).
- Pre-acquisition funding: ~$156M+ across Series A ($31M, 2019), B ($50M, 2020), C ($75M, 2022). Investors: General Catalyst, Mayfield, Capital One Ventures.
