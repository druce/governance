## Key extracted facts

- Red Access **founded 2021**, headquartered in **Tel Aviv, Israel**.
- CEO and co-founder: **Dor Zvi**.
- **$17 million Series A** announced **September 10, 2025**, led by **Norwest Venture Partners**; participants: **Ten Eleven Ventures, SentinelOne's S Ventures, Elron Ventures, Singtel Innov8 Ventures**.
- **Total funding to date: $23 million** (i.e. ~$6M prior seed + $17M Series A).
- Product: **agentless unified security platform** protecting across browsers, applications, and enterprise assets. Markets itself as "the world's first agentless secure browsing platform" and a lightweight SSE (Security Service Edge) alternative without agents or network overhaul.
- Session-based architecture integrates with existing infrastructure (e.g. firewalls), extending SSE capabilities "beyond the perimeter."
- Capabilities: data loss prevention, secure access to corporate assets, protection for remote workers on untrusted networks and unmanaged devices, and securing interactions with GenAI tools.
- Funding use: U.S. expansion, R&D, hiring.
- Quote: "We built Red Access to cut through the noise with fast deployment, seamless integration and uncompromising security that aligns with the way people work today." — Dor Zvi.
