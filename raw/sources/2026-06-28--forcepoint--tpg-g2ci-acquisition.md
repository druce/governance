## Key extracted text

Transaction completed: October 2, 2023. TPG (via TPG Capital) acquired Forcepoint's Global Governments and Critical Infrastructure (G2CI) cybersecurity business from Francisco Partners.

Deal value (press reports): ~$2.45 billion for the G2CI unit.

Business separation:
- G2CI became an independent company (Sean Berg named CEO; Peter Leav Executive Chairman). Francisco Partners retained a MINORITY stake in Forcepoint G2CI.
- COMMERCIAL business: "The Forcepoint Commercial business, focused on simplifying security through a Data-first SASE approach to cloud-delivered security and connectivity, continues to be owned by Francisco Partners and its existing co-investors."

Quote — Andrew Kowal, Francisco Partners: "The closing of this transaction allows both Forcepoint Commercial and G2CI to focus on simplifying security for their primary customers."

Ownership lineage of Forcepoint commercial:
- Raytheon (formed Forcepoint from Websense + Raytheon Cyber Products + Stonesoft) → Francisco Partners acquired Forcepoint from Raytheon in 2021 → 2023 split: TPG takes G2CI (public sector), Francisco Partners keeps Commercial (DLP / Data-first SASE).

=> Today the commercial Forcepoint (DLP / SASE / DSPM) business is owned by Francisco Partners (private equity). Confidence: high.
