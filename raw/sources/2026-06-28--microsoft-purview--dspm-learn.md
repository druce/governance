## Key facts (primary source — Microsoft Learn)
- Microsoft Purview DSPM helps organizations discover, protect, and investigate sensitive data risks across the digital estate. Unified visibility/control for both traditional apps and AI apps & agents.
- Data governance coverage: Microsoft 365, Azure, Fabric, and integrated third-party SaaS. Extended coverage to third-party SaaS/IaaS (Google Cloud Platform, Snowflake, Databricks).
- Consolidates insights from other Purview solutions: Data Loss Prevention (DLP), Insider Risk Management, Information Protection (sensitivity labels), Data Security Investigations. DSPM is the dashboard/posture layer over these.
- Integrates with PARTNER DSPM solutions for risk insights: Varonis, Cyera, BigID, OneTrust. (i.e., Purview can sit alongside / ingest from rival DSPMs.)
- AI/Copilot: dedicated dashboards for AI apps & agents; "AI observability" tracks agent oversharing, exfiltration, unusual access; ready-to-use DLP policies to protect data in AI prompts (Copilot, SharePoint, OneDrive, Teams); endpoint DLP can warn/block sharing to third-party gen-AI sites in browser. Uses Security Copilot + AI triage agents to remediate (remove public links, apply DLP, revoke permissions) under human approval; all actions audited.
- Answers four questions: What data do we have? Where is it stored? Who can access it? How is it protected?
- Licensing: part of Microsoft Purview / M365 E5 compliance stack; requires Purview portal + appropriate Entra/Purview admin roles.

## Ownership
- Microsoft Purview is a Microsoft product (Microsoft Corp, public, NASDAQ: MSFT). Not an independent vendor.
