## Key extracted facts

- CrowdStrike announced **January 13, 2026** a definitive agreement to acquire Seraphic Security, "the leader in browser runtime security."
- Purchase price (per SecurityWeek and CrowdStrike): **$420 million**, paid predominantly in cash with a portion in stock subject to vesting conditions.
- Expected to **close during CrowdStrike's first quarter of FY'27**, subject to customary closing conditions. (As of fetch date, deal is announced/pending, not yet closed.)
- Seraphic CEO and co-founder: **Ilan Yeshua**.
- Product: "continuous in-session browser protection" operating within any browser runtime (Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox), on managed and unmanaged devices; provides "agentless-style" protection for contractors and third parties. Works "without requiring separate secure browsers or rerouting traffic."
- In **January 2025**, Seraphic secured **$29 million Series A** funding, which included investment from the **CrowdStrike Falcon Fund**.
- Strategic rationale: merge Seraphic's browser-native protection with Falcon endpoint telemetry and SGNL's continuous authorization tech into a unified security fabric (endpoint → browser session → cloud). Announced less than a week after CrowdStrike's agreement to acquire identity startup SGNL for $740M.
- Quote: "By decoupling security from the browser itself, we can turn any browser into a secure enterprise browser, without forcing change or slowing productivity." — George Kurtz, CrowdStrike CEO.
